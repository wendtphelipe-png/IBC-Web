Current and Future Directions of the Incisionless Operating Platform

This webinar, broadcast from the International Bariatric Club studios, explores the groundbreaking 'Incisionless Operating Platform' for obesity treatment. A collaboration with the University of Oxford’s Department of Surgical Sciences, the session provides a deep dive into current and future directions of endo-gastrointestinal surgery. Faculty from Germany and beyond share their clinical experience with these advanced technological platforms. The discussion centers on the innovation imperative in laparoscopic and robotic port technology. Experts evaluate the efficacy of incisionless interventions compared to traditional bypass or sleeve procedures. The session highlights the reduction in surgical trauma and the acceleration of patient recovery times. It also addresses the technical nuances of the operating platform and its integration into standard practice. This educational series aims to improve patient care through the dissemination of first-class surgical knowledge. The IBC University of Oxford World Congress 2026 is previewed as a key milestone for this technology. Participants explore the scalability of these incisionless tools in diverse healthcare settings. The webinar serves as a roadmap for surgeons seeking to adopt less invasive metabolic treatments. Each presentation is backed by rigorous clinical data and peer-reviewed research. The Q&A segment tackles the challenges of adoption and long-term outcomes. This content is essential for practitioners at the forefront of surgical innovation. Join the IBC global community in redefining the boundaries of bariatric medicine.
