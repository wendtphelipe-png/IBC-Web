Innovation Imperative: SMART Laparoscopic/Robotic Port Technology

The 131st IBC University of Oxford webinar, titled 'Innovation Imperative: SMART Laparoscopic/Robotic Port Technology', presents a glimpse into the future of surgical access. This collaboration between the IBC and Oxford’s Department of Surgical Sciences features a diverse panel from the USA, UK, Germany, Qatar, and China. Experts discuss the transition from traditional ports to intelligent, sensor-driven access systems. The webinar explores how SMART technology can reduce instrument friction and provide intraoperative feedback. Discussion points include the impact of these innovations on surgical precision and operating room efficiency. The session highlights the rapid evolution of robotic-assisted platforms in bariatric surgery. Faculty evaluate the 'smart' capabilities of next-generation ports in complex gastrointestinal cases. This educational series fulfills the IBC’s mission to provide cutting-edge surgical training globally. The University of Oxford World Congress 2026 is highlighted as the venue for future technological debuts. Participants gain a professional perspective on the implementation of these tools in diverse medical systems. The webinar emphasizes that the 'future is now' for surgeons adopting high-tech access solutions. Each presentation is supported by technical data and expert clinical summaries. the IBC's global audience is invited to explore the frontiers of minimally invasive science. This content is vital for surgeons looking to lead in the era of digital surgery. Join the Oxford-led community in driving the next wave of surgical innovation.
